What the Scoring API Represents

ScoreImpact summarizes how healthy your logging posture is across every service—without Cerbi ever brokering traffic.

CerbiStream governs events at the source, CerbiShield defines and deploys the policies that drive governance signals, and the Scoring API turns those signals into comparable scores while your own routers keep sending logs to the sinks you already use.

Coverage

Tracks how many workloads are under governance, so schema gaps show up before incidents.

Violations

Counts policy breaks like unredacted secrets or dropped samples, weighted by severity and tied to enforcement points.

Redaction Posture

Measures how consistently sensitive fields are masked at the edge and downstream, with CI/runtime coverage signals.

Risk Trends

Shows whether risk is climbing or falling over time, with weight on recent drift and service-specific posture.

Why Scoring Helps

  • Highlights weak spots early, so fixes happen before misconfigurations spill into production.
  • Prioritizes what to fix first by tying scores to the highest-risk services and traffic paths.
  • Provides a common signal during reviews, reducing noisy alerts and surprise outages.
  • Shows governance trends across CI and runtime enforcement so teams can prove improvement over time.

Cross-Service Standardization

  • Uses the same scoring rubric for every team, so policies are enforced evenly without manual checklists.
  • Signals when services diverge from shared baselines, making drift visible without ticket policing.
  • Lets platform teams set defaults once while allowing services to self-correct against the score.
  • Works with any router or collector—CerbiStream and CerbiShield emit governance data, the Scoring API scores it, and your pipelines keep sending logs to your sinks.

FAQ

Is this ML?

No. Scores are rule-based and deterministic so operators can audit every factor.

Does Cerbi store my logs?

No. Cerbi evaluates metadata and policy outcomes; log storage stays in your stack.

Does Cerbi route logs?

No. CerbiStream governs events at the source and CerbiShield manages the policies being enforced, but your own routers forward data to sinks—Cerbi never brokers or forwards log traffic.

Do I need a specific router?

No. Scoring works with any router that can emit governance signals and metrics; the Scoring API is the scoring engine and the legacy CerbIQ-as-router story is retired.
